You didn't choose your personality.

Your nervous system chose it for you — before you had language. Before you had context. Before you had a say.

It picked the version of you most likely to survive that household. That classroom. That relationship.

And you kept it. You built a whole life around it. You defended it in arguments. You put it in your bio.

But it was never who you are.

It was what kept you safe.

And safety is not the same thing as truth.

The real you isn't hiding. It's just waiting for your system to feel safe enough to stop performing.

That shift doesn't come from thinking harder. It comes from showing your body a new experience — and letting it update.

You are not your pattern. You are what remains when the pattern dissolves.
