It is not.

It is something to uncover.

Beneath every pattern of bracing, every loop of hypervigilance, every story that says you must earn your safety — there is a stillness that predates all of it.

Your nervous system knew rest before it knew threat. It knew openness before it learned to wall. The dysregulation was never yours. It was installed. And the body has been waiting — not for a technique, not for a breakthrough — but for the moment you stop long enough to feel what was already there.

Sukūn is not collapse. It is not numbing. It is the original state your system has been trying to return to since the very first time it was pulled away.

You do not need to become regulated. You need to stop rehearsing the reasons you cannot be.
