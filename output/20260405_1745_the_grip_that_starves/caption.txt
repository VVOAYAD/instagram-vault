Protection was intelligent once.

Your body learned to contract. To brace. To grip. And it kept you here.

But the emergency ended years ago.

Now the armor isn't saving you. It's starving you. Every clenched jaw. Every shallow breath. Every guarded yes that should have been an open no.

The nervous system doesn't distinguish between protecting you from danger and protecting you from life. It only knows the pattern.

And the pattern says hold.

So you hold. And you wonder why abundance feels like a theory. Why love gets close and something in you locks the door.

Release is not reckless. Release is the most precise thing you will ever do. It is choosing to let the body complete what the mind interrupted decades ago.

Not thinking your way free. Feeling your way through.

The grip is not keeping you safe. The grip is keeping you small.

Let go — not of everything. Just of the lie that holding is still helping.
