You were never broken. You were buried.

Every defense mechanism in your body was placed there by a moment that overwhelmed you. A moment where the only option was to leave yourself in order to survive yourself.

And it worked. You are still here.

But now the moment is over. And the body has not been told.

So it keeps running programs that belong to rooms you left years ago. Keeps bracing for impacts that already landed. Keeps choosing the familiar wound over the unfamiliar freedom — because to the nervous system, known pain is safer than unknown peace.

Healing was never about becoming someone new. It was always about returning to the one who existed before the first flinch. Before the first mask. Before survival became identity.

That one is still in there. Waiting. Not broken. Buried.

Al-Rajʿah. The Return.

Not backward. Downward. Into the body. Into the breath. Into what is real beneath what was learned.

You do not need to be fixed. You need to be found.
