I spent years confusing shutdown for serenity.

No reactions. No breakdowns. No mess.
I thought I had healed.

But I hadn't arrived in my body.
I had abandoned it so completely that the absence felt like stillness.

The nervous system is designed to protect you. And sometimes the most sophisticated protection it has is to make you feel nothing at all. Not because you're safe. Because it decided feeling was too expensive.

Healing was not learning to stay calm.
It was learning to feel again — and discovering I could survive it.

Real stillness is not empty.
It is full and soft and alive.
You'll know it because you can feel your own heartbeat without flinching.
