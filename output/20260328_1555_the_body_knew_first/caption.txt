Your mind wants to solve the body.

But the body was never the problem.

It was the faithful record keeper — holding every suppressed truth, every swallowed no, every moment you overrode yourself to stay safe.

The tightness in your chest is not dysfunction. It is data.
The knot in your throat is not anxiety. It is a voice.
The collapse in your gut is not weakness. It is an old surrender that never got to complete.

We have been taught that healing means understanding. That if we just find the right insight, the right origin story, the right framework — the pain will dissolve.

But the nervous system doesn't speak in paragraphs. It speaks in pressure. In temperature. In tremor.

And it resolves the same way.

Not through more thinking. Through more feeling.

The moment you stop narrating your experience and start inhabiting it — something ancient begins to move. Something that was waiting for you to stop explaining and start listening.

Your body knew first. It still knows. Let it finish what it started.
