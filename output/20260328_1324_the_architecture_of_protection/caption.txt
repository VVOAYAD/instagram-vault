You didn't build walls because you were broken.
You built them because you were brilliant.

A child who learns the world isn't safe will construct the most sophisticated architecture of protection you've ever seen. Hypervigilance. Emotional withdrawal. Perfectionism. Control.

These aren't dysfunctions. They're masterpieces of survival.

But the nervous system has no calendar. It doesn't know the war ended. So it keeps standing guard at a border no one is crossing anymore.

And slowly — without you noticing — the fortress becomes the prison.

The fracture you're afraid of isn't destruction.
It's the first crack where something real can finally reach you.

You don't need to tear yourself apart.
You just need to stop reinforcing what no longer serves the person you've already become.

The gate was always there.
You built it.
Which means you can open it.
