The transformation you are looking for does not live in the new version of you.

It lives in the space between who you were conditioned to be and who your body has always known you are.

Al-Barzakh. The interval. The sacred pause between the old firing and the familiar collapse.

Your nervous system was not designed to be overridden. It was designed to be met. And when you meet it — in that sliver of space before the pattern completes itself — something ancient in you remembers it has a choice.

This is not mindset work. This is not positive thinking. This is the body reclaiming territory it lost to survival.

The pause is not passive. The pause is where every pattern you have ever carried goes to be seen, felt, and finally released.

Widen the interval. That is the entire practice.
