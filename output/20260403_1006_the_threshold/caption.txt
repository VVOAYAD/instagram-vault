Your body has one alarm system.

It rings for danger. It rings for transformation. Same signal. Same shaking. Same flood of everything unnamed.

So you pull back. You call it anxiety. You call it too much. You assume something is wrong.

Nothing is wrong.

You are standing at the threshold of a self your nervous system has no map for. Of course it resists. Of course it screams. It was built to keep you alive as who you were — not to welcome who you are becoming.

The trembling is not evidence of fragility. It is evidence of encounter. You are meeting the version of you that exists on the other side of everything you were taught to avoid.

Don't medicate the threshold. Don't retreat from the dissolution. Stay with the body. Let it shake. Let it learn that this death is not death.

It is arrival.
