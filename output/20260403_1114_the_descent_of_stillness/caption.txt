You were never dysregulated.

You were defending.

Every locked jaw. Every shallow breath. Every spiral at 2am. That was your nervous system honoring an old agreement — one made in a moment when vigilance meant survival.

But that moment ended. And your body never got the message.

Stillness is not something you build through discipline. It is something that descends when the body finally believes the war is over.

You do not earn it. You do not deserve it. You allow it.

And the allowing is the hardest part — because it asks you to stop doing the only thing that ever kept you safe.

This is the work. Not adding. Releasing. Not fixing. Remembering.

The stillness was never gone. You were just too busy surviving to feel it arrive.
